Kenya Karate Federation

The Fédération Kenyaise de Karaté (KKF) is the official governing body for karate in Kenya. Established to develop, promote, and regulate the sport of karate across the nation, KKF serves as the sole recognized authority for karate activities in the Republic of Kenya.

KKF is affiliated with the World Karate Federation (WKF), the Union of African Karate Federations (UFAK), and operates under the umbrella of the Kenya National Olympic and Sports Committee (NOCK) and the Ministry of Sports.

Our mission is to promote karate as a tool for physical development, character building, and social cohesion while developing competitive athletes who represent Kenya on the continental and international stage.

Our Vision & Mission

Vision: To make Kenya a leading karate nation in Africa, nurturing world-class athletes while promoting the values of respect, discipline, and excellence that define karate.

Mission: To organize, develop, and promote karate at all levels across Kenya — from grassroots youth programs to elite international competition — while ensuring integrity, fair play, and athlete welfare.

Values: Respect (Sonkeranai), Courage (Yuki), Honor (Meiyo), Integrity (Makoto), Discipline (Kiritsu)

Commissions & Committees

Specialized bodies that support the federation's work

🥋 Technical Commission

Oversees technical aspects of karate including coaching standards, grading systems (belt examinations), and competition rules aligned with WKF standards.

⚖️ Referees & Judges Commission

Responsible for training, certifying, and assigning referees and judges for all national and sanctioned competitions following WKF regulations.

👩 Women's Commission

Promotes women's participation in karate across Kenya, from grassroots to elite level. Develops programs to ensure gender equity in the sport.

🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Athletes' Commission

Represents the voice of athletes within the federation, ensuring their rights, welfare, and interests are central to decision-making.

🏥 Medical & Anti-Doping

Ensures athlete health and safety. Implements anti-doping education and testing in cooperation with WADA and Kenya Anti-Doping Organization.

🌱 Youth Development

Develops pathways for young karatekas from school level to junior national team. Coordinates youth leagues and talent identification programs.

Affiliations

KKF is recognized by the following organizations

🌍 World Karate Federation (WKF)

The global governing body for the sport of karate, recognized by the International Olympic Committee. WKF oversees all international karate competitions and sets the rules of the sport.

🌍 Union of African Karate Federations (UFAK)

The continental governing body for karate in Africa. UFAK organizes the African Karate Championships and coordinates karate development across 51 member nations.

🇰🇪 Kenya National Olympic Committee (NOCK)

The national Olympic body that oversees all Olympic sports in Kenya. KKF is a recognized member federation of the NOCK.

🏛️ Ministry of Sports, Kenya

The government ministry responsible for sports policy and development in Kenya. KKF operates within the national sports framework established by the Ministry.

Our History

Key milestones in Kenya karate development

1985

Karate Introduced in Kenya

First karate clubs established in Nairobi, introducing Shotokan karate to Kenyan youth.

1992

KKF Officially Founded

The Kenya Karate Federation is formally established and recognized by the government.

2000

WKF Affiliation

KKF gains official membership with the World Karate Federation, opening doors to international competition.

2010

First African Championship Medal

Kenyan athletes earn their first medals at the UFAK African Karate Championship.

2018

Youth Development Program Launch

National grassroots program launched to develop karate in schools across all provinces.

2026

New Era of Growth

Record 32 clubs, 1200+ athletes, and expanded international participation mark a new chapter for Kenyan karate.
