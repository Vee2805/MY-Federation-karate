My Federation of Karate

The My Federation of Karate (MF) is the official governing body for karate in Our Country. Established to develop, promote, and regulate the sport of karate across the nation, MF serves as the sole recognized authority for karate activities in the Republic of Our Country.

MF is affiliated with the World Karate Federation (WKF), the Union of African Karate Federations (Continental Federation), and operates under the umbrella of the Our Country National Olympic and Sports Committee (National Olympic Committee) and the Ministry of Sports.

Our mission is to promote karate as a tool for physical development, character building, and social cohesion while developing competitive athletes who represent Our Country on the continental and international stage.

Our Vision & Mission

Vision: To make Our Country a leading karate nation in Africa, nurturing world-class athletes while promoting the values of respect, discipline, and excellence that define karate.

Mission: To organize, develop, and promote karate at all levels across Our Country — from grassroots youth programs to elite international competition — while ensuring integrity, fair play, and athlete welfare.

Values: Respect (Sonkeranai), Courage (Yuki), Honor (Meiyo), Integrity (Makoto), Discipline (Kiritsu)

Commissions & Committees

Specialized bodies that support the federation's work

🥋 Technical Commission

Oversees technical aspects of karate including coaching standards, grading systems (belt examinations), and competition rules aligned with WKF standards.

⚖️ Referees & Judges Commission

Responsible for training, certifying, and assigning referees and judges for all national and sanctioned competitions following WKF regulations.

👩 Women's Commission

Promotes women's participation in karate across Our Country, from grassroots to elite level. Develops programs to ensure gender equity in the sport.

🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Athletes' Commission

Represents the voice of athletes within the federation, ensuring their rights, welfare, and interests are central to decision-making.

🏥 Medical & Anti-Doping

Ensures athlete health and safety. Implements anti-doping education and testing in cooperation with WADA and Our Country Anti-Doping Organization.

🌱 Youth Development

Develops pathways for young karatekas from school level to junior national team. Coordinates youth leagues and talent identification programs.

Affiliations

MF is recognized by the following organizations

🌍 World Karate Federation (WKF)

The global governing body for the sport of karate, recognized by the International Olympic Committee. WKF oversees all international karate competitions and sets the rules of the sport.

🌍 Union of African Karate Federations (Continental Federation)

The continental governing body for karate in Africa. Continental Federation organizes the Continental Championships and coordinates karate development across 51 member nations.

Our Country National Olympic Committee (National Olympic Committee)

The national Olympic body that oversees all Olympic sports in Our Country. MF is a recognized member federation of the National Olympic Committee.

🏛️ Ministry of Sports, Our Country

The government ministry responsible for sports policy and development in Our Country. MF operates within the national sports framework established by the Ministry.

Our History

Key milestones in Our Country karate development

1985

Karate Introduced in Our Country

First karate clubs established in Capital City, introducing Shotokan karate to Our Countryn youth.

1992

MF Officially Founded

The My Federation of Karate is formally established and recognized by the government.

2000

WKF Affiliation

MF gains official membership with the World Karate Federation, opening doors to international competition.

2010

First African Championship Medal

Our Countryn athletes earn their first medals at the Continental Federation Continental Championship.

2018

Youth Development Program Launch

National grassroots program launched to develop karate in schools across all provinces.

2026

New Era of Growth

Record 32 clubs, 1200+ athletes, and expanded international participation mark a new chapter for Our Countryn karate.
